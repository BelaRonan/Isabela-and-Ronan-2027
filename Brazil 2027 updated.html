Ouro Preto

Ouro Preto is one of those places that feels like stepping back in time. Tucked into the hills of Minas Gerais, it’s full of winding cobblestone streets, colourful colonial houses, and some of the most beautiful baroque churches in Brazil.

It used to be a major gold mining town, so there’s loads of history around every corner, but it still has a relaxed, lively vibe thanks to its student population and local culture. Think great food, scenic viewpoints, and a slightly chaotic charm that makes it really special.

It's a beautiful city to explore by foot, but bring comfortable shoes.

Where to Stay

  • Centro Histórico (best overall area) – Right in the heart of everything, walkable but steep streets
    • Pousada Solar da Ópera
    • Pousada Clássica
      (Top choice — you’re steps from all main sights)
  • Rosário / Central outskirts (charming & slightly quieter) – Still walkable, but a bit less hectic
    • Hotel Solar do Rosário
    • Hotel Solar de Maria
      (Great balance between location and comfort)
  • Near Historic Entrance (good access + views) – Easy access by car, less hassle with hills
    • Hotel Pousada do Arcanjo
    • Grande Hotel de Ouro Preto
      (Good if you want convenience + iconic stay)

  • São Cristóvão / outskirts (best value & relaxed) – Cheaper, quieter, short drive into centre
    • Hotel Recanto do Ouro
    • Hotel Solar das Lajes
      (Best value for money, but not walkable)

Things to Do

  • Wander the historic centre – Explore cobblestone streets, colourful houses, and hidden viewpoints.

  • Visit Igreja de São Francisco de Assis – Iconic baroque church with incredible detail and artwork.

  • Tour Mina do Chico Rei – Go underground and experience the city’s gold mining history.

  • Try Minas cuisine – Don’t miss Pão de queijo and Feijão tropeiro.

  • Watch sunset at Praça Tiradentes – Best views over the city’s rooftops and hills.

Belo Horizonte

Belo Horizonte is a laid-back but lively city that mixes modern urban life with strong local culture. It’s known for its amazing food scene (seriously, some of the best in Brazil), relaxed vibe, and friendly people.

You’ve got leafy squares, cool architecture around Pampulha, and loads of bars and restaurants to hop between. It’s not as touristy as other big Brazilian cities, which actually makes it feel more authentic—and a great base for exploring Minas Gerais.

Where to Stay

  • Savassi (best overall area) – Lively, central, full of bars & restaurants
    • Holiday Inn Belo Horizonte Savassi
    • Novotel BH Savassi
      (Top choice for tourists and nightlife, very well located)
  • Lourdes (more upscale & relaxed) – Slightly quieter but still central
    • Hotel Fasano Belo Horizonte
    • Mercure Belo Horizonte Lourdes
      (Known as one of the best areas to stay)
  • Pampulha (scenic & more chilled) – Near the lake and architecture, less central
    • Allia Gran Hotel Pampulha Suítes
    • Nobile Inn Pampulha
      (Great if you want views and a quieter stay)

Things to Do

  • Explore Mercado Central de Belo Horizonte – Try local cheeses, meats, and soak up the atmosphere.

  • Visit Pampulha Modern Ensemble – Iconic lakeside architecture by Oscar Niemeyer.

  • Bar-hop in Savassi – Best area for nightlife, bars, and restaurants.

  • Relax at Praça da Liberdade – Beautiful square with gardens and museums.

  • Check out Mercado Novo & botecos – Trendy food, drinks, and a more local, modern vibe.

Rio de Janeiro

Rio really needs no introduction. It’s one of the most iconic cities in the world, where dramatic mountains meet golden beaches and everything feels a bit larger than life.

From the energy of Copacabana and Ipanema to the views from Christ the Redeemer and Sugarloaf Mountain, Rio is all about atmosphere. It’s vibrant, scenic, and full of personality. The perfect place to enjoy sun, sand, sea and samba. 

Where to Stay

  • Ipanema (best overall area) – Safe, stylish, great beach + restaurants
    • Hotel Fasano Rio de Janeiro
    • Sol Ipanema Hotel
      (Top choice — best mix of beach, food, and atmosphere)
  • Copacabana (classic & convenient) – Iconic beach, loads of hotels, slightly more touristy
    • Belmond Copacabana Palace
    • Windsor California Copacabana
      (Great location and easy access to everything)
  • Leblon (upscale & quieter) – More relaxed, safer feel, high-end vibe
    • Janeiro Hotel
    • Sheraton Grand Rio Hotel & Resort
      (Best for a calmer, more premium stay)
  • Santa Teresa (boutique & scenic) – Hillside, artsy, amazing views
    • Santa Teresa Hotel RJ – MGallery
    • Vila Santa Teresa
      (Perfect for something unique and less beach-focused)

Airbnb can also be a great option in Rio, especially in central areas such as Urca, Copacabana, Flamengo

Things to Do

The main and most famous tourist attractions are on the Zona Sul and Centro. So this is where you should stay (because moving in Rio takes time, so better stay there).
  • Visit Christ the Redeemer – Iconic statue with unreal panoramic views.
  • Ride up Sugarloaf Mountain – Cable car views over the whole city and coastline.
  • Relax at Ipanema Beach – Best mix of scenery, vibe, and sunsets.
  • Walk along Copacabana Beach – Famous promenade and buzzing atmosphere.
  • Explore Santa Teresa – Artsy neighbourhood with charm and views.
  • Visit Escadaria Selarón – Colourful tiled steps, great photo spot.
  • Hike Pedra da Gávea or Dois Irmãos – Incredible viewpoints if you’re up for it.
  • Visit Parque Lage – Jungle setting with a cool café and views of Christ.
  • Take a helicopter tour of Rio

Tips and Info

Practical Info

Currency : Brazil uses Brazilian Real (BRL), card is accepted at most places. However some machines won't take foreign cards. It can be good to have some cash on hand, especially for markets, street vendors etc.

Entry Visas : Most of you can enter visa free, a few countries since 2026 must apply for a E-Visa prior to arrival and some may require a physical visa issued, bear in mind visa requirements frequently change, please check the exact requirements and let us know if you need anything.

Power : Brazil uses type N Sockets, so pack a travel adapter.

Phone : eSims can be activated for days/weeks at low cost with Airalo app, to give you data.

Weather : Ouro Preto - ~22–23°C daytime / ~12–13°C night
                 Belo Horizonte - ~24–26°C daytime / ~13–15°C night
                 Rio De Janeiro  - ~25–28°C daytime / ~18–20°C night

Packing : The weather will vary from Minas to Rio, Minas being slightly cooler (Still hot under the sun). Rio can feel very hot and humid, not too cool at night. Pack some light jackets for evening time, and then the usual, summer clothes, sunscreen, sunglasses, hats, lots of swimming suits! And stay hydrated :)

Food & Drink : Brazil has quite a heavy cuisine and eat a lot of meat (Churrasco/Brazilian BBQ is a must try). Brazil is huge and there are many different kinds of food within Brazil. Typical things that you should try: Feijoada (com farofa e couve), Pão de queijo, Coxinha, Pastel, Açaí, Empada/empadão/empadinha, Carne de sol, Churrasco, Acarajé, Tapioca, Brigadeiro, Moqueca, Baião de dois, Bobó de camarão, Churros, Bolinho de bacalhau, Aipim/macaxeira frito, Caipirinha, Original Beer, Cachaca.
                
Language : As most of you probably know, Portuguese is the main language spoken in Brazil, and while you’ll usually get by with English in larger cities, it’s not as widely spoken outside of them. A few basic Portuguese phrases will definitely go a long way and be loved by the locals for the effort. Duolingo can get you through a lot of them.

Safety : Brazil is an amazing place, with amazing people and culture, but like any big destination (More applicable to Rio) there can be risks and areas to avoid, it's not a life or death situation, the main thing to be wary of is pickpocketing and it’s just about using a bit of common sense 😊 It can be a good idea to keep your phone/wallet/keys in a bumbag or cross-body bag, stick to busy areas, avoid flashy valuables, don't wander around deserted areas at night, avoid any trouble and you’ll have an incredible time. Don't let that work up any panic and spoil your fun!

Transport : Best way around in most cities is Uber, it's inexpensive and there are plenty. There are buses, trams and subways. They can be handy but the easiest is Uber.
